The Shining

Author

Stephen King

The Full Story

The Shining tells the chilling story of Jack Torrance, a struggling writer and recovering alcoholic, who takes a winter caretaker job at the isolated Overlook Hotel in the Colorado Rockies. He moves there with his wife Wendy and his young son Danny, who possesses a supernatural ability known as the shining — a psychic gift that lets him see the hotel's horrific past and sense its malevolent forces.

At first, Jack sees the job as a fresh start to rebuild his life and write his novel. However, the hotel's dark history begins to reveal itself. It's a place soaked in evil, haunted by past murders, suicides, and ghostly presences. As the snow traps them in the hotel for the winter, the malevolent forces begin to manipulate Jack, slowly unraveling his sanity.

Meanwhile, Danny experiences terrifying visions of blood-filled elevators, ghostly twins, and a sinister presence called "REDRUM" (murder spelled backward). The hotel's spirit, particularly that of the former caretaker Delbert Grady, begins to possess Jack, encouraging him to "correct" his family.

Jack descends into madness, turning violent and attempting to kill Wendy and Danny with a mallet. Wendy fights back, and Danny uses his psychic ability to reach out telepathically to Dick Hallorann, a cook who also "shines" and had warned Danny about the hotel's evil. Hallorann rushes back in a snowstorm to help.

In the end, Danny's courage and love for his father briefly break through Jack's madness. Jack regains clarity for a moment but ultimately dies when the hotel's neglected boiler explodes, destroying the Overlook and ending its terror. Wendy and Danny survive with Hallorann's help.

Moral

Unchecked anger and isolation can destroy, but love, courage, and inner strength can overcome even the darkest forces.
